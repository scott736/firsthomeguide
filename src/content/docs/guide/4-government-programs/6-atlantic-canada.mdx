Atlantic Canada — Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, and Newfoundland and Labrador — offers some of the most affordable housing in the country, combined with targeted programs that make [first-time homeownership](/guide/1-are-you-ready/1-first-time-buyer/) even more accessible. Nova Scotia's down payment assistance program is one of the most direct in Canada, and PEI offers a full property transfer tax exemption for first-time buyers.

---

## Nova Scotia

Nova Scotia offers two standout programs for first-time buyers: an interest-free down payment loan and an HST rebate on new construction. Combined with some of the lowest average home prices among HST provinces, Nova Scotia is an increasingly attractive market for first-time buyers.

### Down Payment Assistance Program (DPAP)

Nova Scotia's Down Payment Assistance Program provides an **interest-free loan of 5% of the purchase price** to help first-time buyers cover their minimum down payment.

**Key details:**

- **Loan amount:** 5% of the purchase price (matching the minimum down payment requirement)
- **Interest rate:** **0%** — the loan is completely interest-free
- **Repayment:** The loan must be repaid over a **10-year period**
- **Purchase price caps:** The maximum purchase price varies by region — **$570,000** in Halifax Regional Municipality, **$375,000** in Annapolis/West Hants/South Shore, and **$300,000** in Yarmouth and Northern/Eastern regions
- **Household income limit:** You must have a household income under the program threshold (check with the Nova Scotia government for current figures)
- **Combinable with federal programs:** You can use the down payment loan alongside your FHSA, HBP, and other savings

**Example: $300,000 home in Halifax**
- Minimum down payment needed: $300,000 x 5% = $15,000
- Nova Scotia Down Payment Assistance: **$15,000 interest-free loan**
- Your personal savings needed for down payment: **$0** (the loan covers the full minimum)
- You still need savings for closing costs (approximately $4,500 to $9,000 depending on legal fees, home inspection, title insurance, etc.)

### First-Time Homebuyers Program (NEW — February 2026)

Nova Scotia launched a groundbreaking **4-year pilot program** in February 2026 that reduces the minimum down payment from 5% to just **2%** for qualifying first-time buyers — making it the first province in Canada to do so.

**Key details:**

- **Down payment:** Only **2%** of the purchase price required (vs. the standard 5%)
- **Delivered through:** Participating credit unions
- **Government guarantee:** The province guarantees the mortgages, so no CMHC mortgage insurance is required
- **Interest rate:** Capped at **prime + 2%**
- **Purchase price caps:** **$570,000** in Halifax Regional Municipality and East Hants; **$500,000** elsewhere in the province
- **Income limit:** Household income of **$200,000 or less**
- **Credit score:** Minimum **630**
- **Eligibility:** Must pass the CMHC stress test; must be a Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or have a provincial immigration endorsement

**Example: $400,000 home in Halifax**
- Standard 5% down payment: $20,000
- With this program (2% down): **$8,000**
- **Savings: $12,000 less needed upfront**

:::tip
Nova Scotia now offers two powerful paths for first-time buyers: the DPAP (interest-free 5% loan) and the new 2% down payment program. Depending on your situation, one may be better than the other. The DPAP covers the full 5% down payment with a loan, while the new program simply requires less down payment upfront. Contact a participating credit union or the Nova Scotia Department of Municipal Affairs and Housing to determine which is right for you.
:::

### Nova Scotia HST Rebate on New Homes

Buyers of newly constructed homes in Nova Scotia may be eligible for a rebate on the **provincial portion of the HST**. Nova Scotia's HST rate is 15% (5% federal + 10% provincial).

**Key details:**

- The provincial rebate is **18.75% of the provincial HST paid**
- Maximum rebate: approximately **$3,000** (on homes priced up to approximately $400,000)
- Available to all buyers, not just first-time buyers
- The home must be your primary residence
- Combined with the federal GST rebate (up to $6,300), the total new home tax rebate in Nova Scotia can reach approximately **$9,300**

### [Nova Scotia Property Transfer Tax](https://www.novascotia.ca/sns/access/land/land-transfer-tax.asp)

Nova Scotia charges a **Deed Transfer Tax** set by each municipality. In Halifax, the rate is **1.5% of the purchase price**. Other municipalities may have different rates.

**Example: $350,000 home in Halifax**
- Deed Transfer Tax: $350,000 x 1.5% = **$5,250**

There is **no provincial first-time buyer rebate** on the Deed Transfer Tax in Nova Scotia. However, some municipalities may offer their own incentives — check with your local municipality.

## New Brunswick

New Brunswick offers a relatively straightforward set of programs for home buyers, with one of the lowest property transfer tax rates in Canada and an HST rebate for new construction.

### HST Rebate on New Homes

New Brunswick offers a rebate on the provincial portion of the HST for qualifying new home purchases. New Brunswick's HST rate is 15% (5% federal + 10% provincial).

**Key details:**

- Maximum provincial rebate: up to **$3,000**
- Applies to new construction and **substantially renovated homes**
- Available to **all buyers**, not just first-time buyers
- The home must be your **primary residence**
- Combined with the federal GST rebate (up to $6,300), the total potential rebate is up to **$9,300**

### [New Brunswick Property Transfer Tax](https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/departments/finance/taxes/real_property_transfer.html)

New Brunswick charges a **Real Property Transfer Tax** at a flat rate of **1% of the assessed value or purchase price**, whichever is greater. This is one of the **lowest property transfer tax rates** in Canada.

**Example: $300,000 home in Moncton**
- Property Transfer Tax: $300,000 x 1% = **$3,000**

There is **no first-time buyer rebate** on the property transfer tax in New Brunswick. However, the low 1% rate means the tax is already relatively modest compared to provinces like Ontario and BC.

### New Brunswick Homeownership Programs

New Brunswick periodically offers housing assistance programs through the New Brunswick Housing Corporation. These may include:

- **Home renovation assistance** for qualifying low-income homeowners
- **Affordable housing initiatives** in various municipalities
- **Down payment assistance** for qualifying low-income buyers (income-tested)

Check with the New Brunswick Housing Corporation for current program availability.

## Prince Edward Island

PEI offers one of the simplest and most valuable first-time buyer programs in Canada: a **complete exemption** from the property transfer tax.

### [Real Property Transfer Tax Exemption](https://www.princeedwardisland.ca/en/information/finance/real-property-transfer-tax)

PEI normally charges a Real Property Transfer Tax of **1% of the greater of the purchase price or the assessed value**. First-time home buyers are **fully exempt** from this tax.

**Key details:**

- **Full exemption:** First-time buyers pay **$0** in property transfer tax
- You must be a **first-time home buyer** (have never owned residential property)
- The property must be your **principal residence**
- You must be a **Canadian citizen or permanent resident**

**Savings examples:**

| Purchase Price | PTT for Non-FTHB | FTHB Savings |
|---------------|-------------------|--------------|
| $200,000 | $2,000 | **$2,000** |
| $300,000 | $3,000 | **$3,000** |
| $400,000 | $4,000 | **$4,000** |
| $500,000 | $5,000 | **$5,000** |

### Down Payment Assistance Program (NEW — Pilot)

PEI now offers a **Down Payment Assistance Program (DPAP)** pilot providing an **interest-free loan of up to 5% of the purchase price**, with a maximum of **$17,500**, to help first-time buyers cover their down payment. The pilot runs until March 31, 2026, or until the allocated budget is committed. Contact the PEI government for current availability and eligibility requirements.

### PEI Homeownership and Market Advantages

PEI offers additional advantages for first-time buyers beyond the PTT exemption and new DPAP:

- **Lower home prices** — PEI has some of the most affordable homes in Canada, with average prices well below the national average
- **Growing economy** — PEI's economy has been growing steadily, driven by immigration, tourism, and technology sectors
- **Tight rental market** — High rental costs relative to mortgage payments make homeownership especially attractive in PEI

:::tip
PEI's combination of a full property transfer tax exemption, lower home prices, and a growing economy makes it one of the most accessible provinces in Canada for first-time homeownership. A first-time buyer can often purchase a home in Charlottetown for $250,000 to $350,000 — prices that are a fraction of what comparable homes cost in Toronto or Vancouver.
:::

## Newfoundland and Labrador

Newfoundland and Labrador offers down payment assistance and HST rebates to support first-time buyers, with some of the most affordable home prices in the country.

### First-Time Homebuyers Program (FHP)

The Newfoundland and Labrador Housing Corporation operates the **First-Time Homebuyers Program (FHP)** that provides both a down payment loan and a closing cost grant to qualifying buyers.

**Down payment loan:**

- **Loan amount:** 5% of the purchase price, up to **$17,500** in St. John's and Labrador, **$15,000** in regional centres (Clarenville, Gander, Grand Falls-Windsor, Corner Brook, Stephenville and communities within 30 km), and **$12,500** in rural areas
- **Interest:** The loan is **not** interest-free (unlike Nova Scotia's DPAP)
- **Repayment:** No repayment required for the first 5 years after purchase
- **Maximum purchase prices:** **$350,000** in St. John's and Labrador, **$300,000** in regional centres, **$250,000** in the rest of the province (with a 10% variance policy available upon approval)

**Closing cost grant:**

- Up to **$1,500** (50% of legal closing costs) — provided as a grant, not a loan

**Income eligibility:**

- Household income must be under **$95,000** for the full amount; reduced amounts available for incomes up to $95,000
- Households earning **$85,000 or less** receive the maximum assistance
- Can be combined with federal programs ([FHSA](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/individuals/topics/first-home-savings-account.html), [HBP](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/individuals/topics/rrsps-related-plans/what-home-buyers-plan.html), HBTC)
- Contact the [**Newfoundland and Labrador Housing Corporation**](https://www.nlhc.nl.ca/housing-programs/first-time-homebuyers-program-fhp/) for current program details and application deadlines

### HST Rebate on New Construction

As an HST province (rate of 15% — 5% federal + 10% provincial), Newfoundland and Labrador offers a rebate on the provincial portion of the HST for new home construction.

**Key details:**

- The provincial rebate provides a percentage of the 10% provincial HST back to the buyer
- Available on newly built homes intended as the buyer's primary residence
- Combined with the federal GST rebate (up to $6,300), this provides meaningful savings on new construction
- Check with the Newfoundland and Labrador government for current rebate rates and thresholds

### Newfoundland Registration of Deeds Tax

Newfoundland charges a **Registration of Deeds Tax** on property purchases. The rates are modest:

- **$100 plus $0.40 per $100** of the property's value

**Example: $250,000 home in St. John's**
- Registration tax: $100 + ($250,000 / $100 x $0.40) = $100 + $1,000 = **$1,100**

There is no first-time buyer exemption on this tax, but the amount is relatively low compared to other provinces.
