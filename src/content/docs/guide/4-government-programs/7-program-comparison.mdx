## Federal Programs (Available Everywhere)

These programs are available to all qualifying first-time home buyers across Canada, regardless of province. For full details on each program, see the [federal programs page](/guide/4-government-programs/1-federal-programs/):

| Program | Benefit | Key Details |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **[First Home Savings Account (FHSA)](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/individuals/topics/first-home-savings-account.html)** | Tax-deductible contributions + tax-free withdrawals | Up to $40,000 lifetime; $8,000/year; double tax advantage |
| **[RRSP Home Buyers' Plan (HBP)](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/individuals/topics/rrsps-related-plans/what-home-buyers-plan.html)** | Tax-free RRSP withdrawal for home purchase | Up to $60,000/person ($120,000/couple); repay over 15 years |
| **[Home Buyers' Tax Credit (HBTC)](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/individuals/topics/about-your-tax-return/tax-return/completing-a-tax-return/deductions-credits-expenses/line-31270-home-buyers-amount.html)** | $1,500 non-refundable tax credit | Claim on line 31270 of your tax return the year you buy |
| **[GST/HST New Housing Rebate](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/forms-publications/publications/rc4028/gst-hst-new-housing-rebate.html)** | Up to $6,300 federal rebate on new construction | Homes up to $450,000; partial rebate $350K-$450K |
| **GST Exemption (enacted March 2026)** | Full GST elimination for first-time buyers on new builds under $1M | First-time buyers only; saves up to ~$50,000; phased out $1M-$1.5M |

## Stacking Programs: Combined Savings Examples

The real power of these programs comes from stacking multiple federal and provincial benefits together. Here are detailed examples for buyers in different provinces.

### Example 1: First-Time Buyer in Toronto, Ontario — $600,000 Resale Condo

Note: If you are buying a **new build** in Ontario instead of resale, the federal GST is eliminated (confirmed law). The Ontario provincial HST elimination has been announced but is pending provincial legislation — if fully enacted, combined savings could be up to $130,000 on homes under $1M. The example below covers resale.



| Program | Savings |
|---------|---------|
| FHSA tax deductions (5 years x $8,000 at 30% marginal rate) | $12,000 |
| Ontario Provincial LTT Rebate | $4,000 |
| Toronto Municipal LTT Rebate | $4,475 |
| Home Buyers' Tax Credit (HBTC) | $1,500 |
| **Total savings** | **$21,975** |

Additionally, the buyer could access up to $60,000 from their RRSP through the HBP (tax-free at withdrawal, but must be repaid over 15 years) and up to $40,000 from their FHSA (truly tax-free with no repayment). Combined, that is up to **$100,000** from registered accounts available for the down payment and closing costs — plus the $21,975 in non-repayable savings above.

### Example 2: First-Time Buyer in Vancouver, BC — $700,000 Condo

| Program | Savings |
|---------|---------|
| FHSA tax deductions (5 years x $8,000 at 30% marginal rate) | $12,000 |
| BC First-Time Buyer PTT Exemption | $12,000 |
| Home Buyers' Tax Credit (HBTC) | $1,500 |
| BC Home Owner Grant (first year) | $570 |
| **Total first-year savings** | **$26,070** |

Over 10 years of claiming the BC Home Owner Grant, total savings climb to approximately **$31,770**.

### Example 3: First-Time Buyer in Calgary, Alberta — $420,000 Townhouse

| Program | Savings |
|---------|---------|
| FHSA tax deductions (5 years x $8,000 at 30% marginal rate) | $12,000 |
| No land transfer tax (registration fee only ~$400 vs. $5,475 Ontario LTT on same home) | ~$5,075 saved vs. Ontario |
| Home Buyers' Tax Credit (HBTC) | $1,500 |
| **Total direct savings** | **$13,500** |

While Alberta has fewer named programs, the absence of land transfer tax and PST provides structural savings that benefit every buyer, every year.

### Example 4: First-Time Buyer in Montreal, Quebec — $450,000 New Build

| Program | Savings |
|---------|---------|
| FHSA tax deductions (5 years x $8,000 at 30% marginal rate) | $12,000 |
| Montreal Home Ownership Grant | Up to $15,000 |
| Home Buyers' Tax Credit (HBTC) | $1,500 |
| Federal GST Rebate (new construction) | Up to $6,300 |
| Quebec QST Rebate (new construction) | Up to $9,975 |
| **Total potential savings** | **Up to $44,775** |

Note: The QST rebate is clawed back on homes above approximately $277,500 (pre-tax), so the actual rebate would be lower on a $450,000 home. The welcome tax of approximately $5,300 must also be paid at closing.

### Example 5: First-Time Buyer in Halifax, Nova Scotia — $300,000 Home

| Program | Savings |
|---------|---------|
| FHSA tax deductions (5 years x $8,000 at 30% marginal rate) | $12,000 |
| Nova Scotia Down Payment Assistance (5% interest-free loan) | $15,000 (loan, not savings) |
| Home Buyers' Tax Credit (HBTC) | $1,500 |
| **Total direct savings (excluding loan)** | **$13,500** |
| **Total financial benefit (including down payment loan)** | **$28,500** |

The $15,000 interest-free loan is not "savings" in the traditional sense — you must repay it eventually — but it eliminates the need to save for a down payment entirely, which can accelerate your home purchase by years.

## Key Takeaways

:::tip
**Do not leave money on the table.** Many of these programs must be actively claimed — they are not applied automatically. Here is who helps you claim each type:

- **Land transfer tax rebates** — Your real estate **lawyer** claims these at closing. Make sure they know you are a first-time buyer.
- **HBTC ($1,500 credit)** — You or your **accountant** claims this on your tax return (line 31270) for the year you purchased.
- **FHSA deductions** — You or your **accountant** claims these on your tax return each year you contribute.
- **GST/HST rebates** — Either your **builder** applies them to the purchase price, or you claim them directly through the CRA after closing. Ask your builder which approach they use.
- **Provincial down payment assistance** — You apply **before or during** the purchase process through the provincial housing authority.
- **Municipal grants (Montreal, etc.)** — You apply through the **municipal government**, often before closing.

Work with your lawyer, accountant, and mortgage broker to create a checklist of every program you are eligible for. Assign responsibility for each one. Missing a single program because nobody remembered to apply is an expensive mistake.
:::

:::caution
Program rules, income limits, and grant amounts change frequently. The information in this guide is current as of early 2026, but you should always verify the details directly with the relevant government website or program administrator before making financial decisions based on these programs. Your mortgage broker and real estate lawyer should also be up to date on the latest rules.
:::
