You did it. The keys are in your hand, and this home is yours. After months (maybe years) of planning, saving, searching, and negotiating, you are officially a Canadian homeowner.

But homeownership is not just a destination — it is an ongoing responsibility. The decisions you make in the months and years after closing will determine whether your home becomes a source of financial security or a source of stress.

---

## What This Module Covers

### [1. Your Emergency Fund](/guide/8-life-after-closing/1-emergency-fund/)

Why homeowners need 3-6 months of living expenses saved, what major repairs can cost ($3,500-$20,000+), and the 1-3% rule for annual maintenance budgeting.

### [2. Property Taxes](/guide/8-life-after-closing/2-property-taxes/)

How property taxes are assessed, when they are due by province, how to set up monthly payment plans, and the education tax portion.

### [3. Home Insurance](/guide/8-life-after-closing/3-home-insurance/)

What standard policies cover and do not cover, the essential add-ons (sewer backup, overland flooding), how to shop for insurance, typical costs ($1,000-$2,500/year), and bundling discounts.

### [4. Mortgage Renewal Strategy](/guide/8-life-after-closing/4-mortgage-renewal/)

Why you should never auto-renew, how to shop 120 days before your term ends, when switching lenders makes sense, and when to break your mortgage early. If you need a refresher on how interest rates work, revisit [Module 3: Fixed vs. Variable Rates](/guide/3-down-payments-mortgages/3-fixed-vs-variable/).

### [5. Home Maintenance](/guide/8-life-after-closing/5-home-maintenance/)

The 1% rule for annual maintenance budgeting, a seasonal maintenance checklist (spring/summer/fall/winter), and when to DIY vs. hire professionals.

### [6. Energy Efficiency Upgrades & Grants](/guide/8-life-after-closing/6-energy-efficiency/)

Canada Greener Homes programs, provincial energy incentives, common upgrades and their ROI, and the EnerGuide audit process.

### [7. Building Home Equity](/guide/8-life-after-closing/7-building-equity/)

How equity grows over time, accelerated payment strategies that save tens of thousands in interest, lump sum payment privileges, and when to refinance.

---

## Key Numbers for New Homeowners

| Guideline | Amount |
|---|---|
| Emergency fund | 3-6 months of living expenses |
| Annual maintenance budget | 1-3% of home value |
| Mortgage renewal shopping | Start 120 days before term ends |
| Home insurance | $1,000-$2,500/year |
| Accelerated bi-weekly savings | ~$49,000 in interest on a $400K mortgage |
